Glencore Corporate Practice

Glencore Corporate Practice, or GCP, is our corporate framework and programme for non-financial aspects of our performance. GCP covers:

  • Health & Safety
  • Environment
  • Community relations
  • Human Resources
  • Our impact on society and local economies
  • Compliance

GCP aims to ensure the highest level of business conduct across the Glencore group. We also use the GCP principles, commitments and reporting requirements to promote understanding and acceptance of how we manage sustainability. This extends to both internal and external stakeholders.

We also support the Extractive Industries Transparency Initiative (EITI). Glencore currently operates industrial assets in four EITI candidate countries: the Democratic Republic of Congo, Kazakhstan, Peru and Zambia. EITI supporting companies take part in EITI’s global governance, through their country operations (in implementing countries) and through industry associations.

Experience and consultation

We determined the principles on which GCP is based, using our experience of operating our assets, our marketing activities, and our interactions with our stakeholders. We followed a strict set of criteria during selection:

  • The impact it has on our business and the regions where we work
  • The degree to which it is under our direct control or indirect influence
  • Its potential impact on our people, local communities, customers, operations or sourcing
  • Its relevance to our key stakeholders

We also considered international and sector-specific standards and guidelines, our own reporting and risk management systems, our community and investor relations programmes and the outcomes of regular dialogue between our corporate management and operational staff.

GCP components

The GCP framework and basic requirements already apply, across all our business groups and commodity departments. The full GCP programme of targets and objectives will be completed in 2011.

Global and local engagement

Managing sustainability professionally and securely requires corporate involvement for guidance, facilitation, control, active support and communication. But local operational responsibility is the decisive factor in our success.
