E&P

As part of its E&P portfolio Glencore has equity stakes in two oil and gas Production Sharing Contracts offshore Equatorial Guinea, West Africa (Block I and Block O). Both Blocks are operated by US based Noble Energy Inc.

Since 2005 a programme of exploration and appraisal drilling on the two blocks has resulted in the discovery of significant oil, condensate and gas reserves.

One of these oil discoveries in Block I, the Aseng Field, is under development with first oil expected early 2012. The development will comprise a sub-sea tie-back of production wells to a fixed Floating Production, Storage and Off-take vessel ("FPSO") with oil export by tanker off-take. At its peak Aseng will produce 50,000 barrels of oil a day from five production wells.

A second gas/condensate discovery in Block O, the Alen Field, is also under development with first production expected early 2014. The development will comprise two bridge-linked platforms (a wellhead platform and a central processing platform), gas will initially be delivered to the platform where it will stripped of condensate and re-injected back into the field via three subsea injection wells. The condensate liquids will be sent via pipeline to the Aseng FPSO for storage and off-take. At its peak the Alen Field will produce approximately 33,000 barrels of oil a day from three production wells.
