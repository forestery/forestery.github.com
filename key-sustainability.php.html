Key sustainability issues

During the creation of the GCP framework, we included a range of considerations. As Glencore is a large group with operations covering hugely differing activities, we found that the matters to be addressed fell into different categories. Some were relevant to Glencore as a group, while others affected individual operations or divisions.

Organisation-wide issues

Our utmost responsibility, across every area of the Glencore group, is to our people. Any sustainability programme must consider both their immediate Health & Safety and their overall wellbeing and satisfaction.

Other matters for group-wide consideration are, in no particular order:

  • Compliance with international and local regulations
  • Respect for human rights in all interactions with our people, business partners and society
  • The global challenges of climate change
  • Our contribution to both the world-wide economy and the economies of local communities

Marketing activities

This is how we refer to our trading and sales activities, as well as the infrastructure and resources needed to store and transport our products. Our reputation with our clients is key to these activities, and is based on the quality and reliability of our sales and logistics functions. This makes quality a material consideration at all times and the most important for our wide range of marketing activities.

Other issues for our marketing division include:

  • Maintaining reliable, safe and timely operations
  • Compliance with all safety and transport regulations
  • Maintaining supply chain integrity:
    • Purchase and sales sides for regulatory compliance
    • Supply chain sustainability for customers and regulators

Industrial activities

For Glencore this is mostly mining, metallurgical smelting and refining, operating agricultural processing plants or agricultural production itself. These operations come with specific occupational health challenges as well as occupational and industrial safety issues. These will always be top priorities, as we must protect our people and communities from harm and our operations from business interruptions.

Complex operations such as mines or smelters have environmental impacts that include:

  • Air emissions
  • Industrial and mining waste
  • Fresh water use
  • Related waste water issues
  • Possible negative impact on biodiversity

Our local communities benefit from our activities in terms of employment and improved infrastructure (eg public supplies or medical care), but adverse effects cannot always be prevented, which makes adequate mitigation programmes another important material factor.
