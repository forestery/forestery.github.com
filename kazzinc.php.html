Kazzinc

Kazzinc is a major fully integrated zinc producer, with considerable copper, precious metals and lead credits. The company was established in 1997 through the merger of Eastern Kazakhstan's three main non-ferrous metals companies. The three were majority Government-owned, together with the Bukhtarma Hydro-Electric Power station which provides sufficient power to cover all Kazzinc's energy needs. Since the acquisition by Glencore's subsidiaries in 1997, the output of all the major products has increased by more than 50%.
Kazzinc also owns 100% of the Vasilkovskoe Gold mine in Kazakhstan and 48.3% of the Novoshirokinskoe Gold mine in Russia.
Kazzinc is currently in the process of spinning off its Gold assets into a separate vehicle, allowing it to focus on the development of its precious metals business as core commodities.
